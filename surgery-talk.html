Head & Neck May 14, 2026

Thyroid surgery is deceptively common and unforgivingly unforgiving. A single misidentified recurrent laryngeal nerve ends a career and permanently damages a patient. This breakdown covers the anatomy, operative strategy, and decision-making that separates a competent thyroidectomist from a dangerous one.

  • RLN anatomy: Why the right RLN loops around the subclavian (non-recurrent variant in 0.5%) while the left loops the aortic arch — and why this matters intraoperatively

  • Parathyroid preservation: Identifying all 4 glands, the inferior parathyroid blood supply, and the auto-transplantation decision
