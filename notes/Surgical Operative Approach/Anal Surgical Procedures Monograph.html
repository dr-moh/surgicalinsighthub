Anal Surgical Procedures

Haemorrhoidectomy, Fistula, Fissure, and Abscess: The precision of anorectal restoration.

PILLAR 1 Decision Framework

1.1 Clinical Workup

  • Haemorrhoids: Grade III (manual reduction) and IV (irreducible) are surgical.
  • Fistula: Rule out Crohn's disease before any definitive cutting.
  • Fissure: Chronic (>6 weeks) failing medical therapy (GTN/Diltiazem).
PHILOSOPHY: Permanent incontinence is one of the most consequential complications in surgery. Protect the sphincter at all costs.
PILLAR 2 The Operative Game Plan

2.2 Haemorrhoidectomy (Milligan-Morgan)

  • Rule of Three: Preserve at least 3 skin-mucosal bridges of 5mm width to prevent anal stenosis.
  • Technique: Identify and preserve the internal sphincter (white fibres) during dissection.

2.4 Fistula-in-Ano (Goodsall's Rule)

  • Anterior: Usually track straight to the anal canal.
  • Posterior: Track to the posterior midline (curved).
  • Management: Fistulotomy for low tracts; Seton for high/complex tracts.
PILLAR 3 Technical Pearls
Consultant Pearl: The "white line of Hilton" marks the lower edge of the internal sphincter. Identifying this is the difference between a successful LIS and a disaster.
