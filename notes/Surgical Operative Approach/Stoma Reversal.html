Stoma Reversal

Hartmann's, Loop Ileostomy, and Colostomy: The restoration of intestinal continuity.

PILLAR 1 Pre-operative Optimization

1.1 Clinical Workup

  • Original Indication: Malignancy, diverticulitis, IBD, or trauma? Review original op note.
  • Interval: Minimum 8-12 weeks to allow pelvic adhesions to mature/soften.
  • Anorectal Function: Confirm continence before reversing Hartmann's.

1.2 Distal Limb Assessment

MANDATORY: Water-soluble contrast enema (Gastrografin) AND Flexible sigmoidoscopy to exclude stricture or disuse atrophy.
PILLAR 2 The Operative Game Plan

2.3 Hartmann's Reversal (Major Restoration)

  • Access: Midline laparotomy usually required. Splenic flexure mobilization often necessary.
  • Technique: Circular stapled end-to-end. Full-thickness purse-string is critical.
  • Leak Test: Air insufflation per rectum with pelvis filled with saline.
PILLAR 3 Technical Pearls & Bail-Outs
Consultant Pearl: Purse-string skin closure at the stoma site significantly reduces SSI compared to primary closure. Leave it to granulate.
