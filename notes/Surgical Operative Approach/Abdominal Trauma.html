Abdominal Trauma

Residency Journal  ·  Trauma Surgery  ·  AAST OIS 2024
Page 1 / 12
Brutal Rule
Control the renal vascular pedicle BEFORE opening Gerota's fascia. Opening Gerota's in a high-grade injury without proximal control is how you lose a kidney to torrential haemorrhage.
1.1

Pathophysiology: Spleen

The spleen is the most commonly injured solid organ in blunt abdominal trauma (25-30%). Left lower rib fractures (9-11) are present in 40% of cases.
  • Anatomy: 150-200g. Tethered by gastrosplenic, lienorenal, phrenocolic, and splenocolic ligaments.
  • Danger Zone: Pancreatic tail is within 1cm of the splenic hilum in 75% of people.
  • Physiology: Receives 5% of cardiac output. Risk of OPSI (Overwhelming Post-Splenectomy Infection) is lifetime (3-5%).
1.2

Pathophysiology: Kidney

Kidneys are the most frequently injured GU organ. 80-85% are blunt trauma.
  • Location: Retroperitoneal at T12-L3. Bleeding is contained by Gerota's fascia initially.
  • Haematuria: cardinal sign in 90%, but does NOT correlate with injury grade. 25% of Grade V pedicle avulsions have NO haematuria.

Renal Imaging Algorithm

Image if: Gross haematuria OR Microscopic haematuria + instability OR High-energy mechanism.
Never Miss

Delayed Phase (7-10 min) is mandatory on CT to rule out collecting system disruption (Grade IV).

3.1

Splenectomy Steps

  1. Access: Midline laparotomy. Manual compression of spleen.
  2. Mobilisation: Divide phrenocolic and splenorenal ligaments. Deliver spleen medially.
  3. Short Gastrics: Divide between ligatures carefully.
  4. Hilar Control: Ligate artery then vein. Watch the Pancreas!
3.2

Renal Exploration

Principle: Proximally control the renal artery/vein at the aorta BEFORE opening the Zone II haematoma.
  • Left Renal Vein: Landmark crossing the aorta.
  • One-Shot IVP: Confirm contralateral function before nephrectomy.

5.1

Viva Scenarios

Q: Post-splenectomy fever 40°C, rigor, BP 80/50.
A: OPSI. Emergency. Ceftriaxone 2g IV immediately. 50% mortality.
Vaccine Timing

Administer PCV13, PPSV23, MenACWY, MenB, and Hib on Day 14 post-op. Not earlier!
