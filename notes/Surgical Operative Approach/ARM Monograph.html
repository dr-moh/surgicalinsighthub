Anorectal Malformation

Residency Journal  ·  Pediatric Surgery  ·  Krickenbeck 2005
Page 1 / 7
Brutal Rule
Classify every ARM by Krickenbeck before touching the patient — the operative approach is dictated entirely by the type, and operating without knowing the fistula level is how you destroy a sphincter complex that was never going to be repaired again.
01

Definition & Embryology

ARM = failure of the urorectal septum to descend completely by week 7 of gestation, leaving the hindgut and urogenital sinus incompletely separated.

The Sacral Ratio

Quantifies nerve supply and predicts continence. Calculated on AP/Lateral X-rays.
RatioPrognosis
> 0.7Good Continence Likely
0.4 - 0.7Variable / Borderline
< 0.4Poor — mandatory bowel management
Never Miss

MRI spine is mandatory before PSARP to rule out tethered cord (25-35%). Prone positioning can cause cord injury if tethered.

Newborn Assessment

04

The 24-Hour Rule

Wait 24 hours before deciding on the operation. Meconium takes time to track through the fistula.
FindingSignificance
Meconium in UrineRectourethral fistula (High ARM)
Single Opening (F)Cloaca
Meconium on PerineumPerineal fistula (Low ARM)
05

VACTERL Screening

  • Echo first — cardiac anomalies are immediately lethal.
  • Renal US — 40-50% have renal anomalies.
  • Distal Colostogram — Gold standard before PSARP.

Operative Strategy

Page 4 / 7
06

Divided Sigmoid Colostomy

Mandatory for high ARMs. Loop colostomy is contraindicated as it leads to UTI and urosepsis.
07

PSARP (Peña Technique)

  1. Prone jackknife position.
  2. Posterior sagittal midline incision.
  3. Muscle stimulator mapping of sphincter complex.
  4. Division of fistula and rectal mobilization.
  5. Precise placement of rectum within the muscle complex.
