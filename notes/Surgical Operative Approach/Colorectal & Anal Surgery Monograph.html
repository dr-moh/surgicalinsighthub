1.3 Board Review / Viva

Viva: 16-year-old, APC mutation, 200+ adenomas, 12 rectal polyps. What operation?

Answer: Proctocolectomy + IPAA. IRA acceptable only if <20 rectal polyps and patient strongly prefers; requires 6-monthly flexible sigmoidoscopy for life.

MONOGRAPH 2 Colon Cancer (CRC)

2.1 Operative Atlas: Complete Mesocolic Excision

  • Toldt's fascia: embryological fusion plane: the correct CME dissection plane.
  • Hohenberger 2009: Sharp dissection in embryological fascial planes + central vascular ligation at mesenteric root (D3).

3.2 Neoadjuvant & TNT Trials

RAPIDO (2021): TNT (SCRT + CAPOX) reduced disease-related treatment failure (23.7% vs 30.4%). pCR 28% vs 14%.
OPRA (2022): Consolidation chemotherapy after LCRT → higher cCR and organ preservation (58%).
MONOGRAPH 4 CRLM & Peritoneal Disease

4.1 Resectability & FLR

  • Adequate FLR: ≥20–25% normal liver; ≥30–40% post-chemo.
  • PVE: Embolise portal vein to tumour-bearing lobe → contralateral hypertrophy (4–6 weeks).

4.3 CRS / HIPEC

PRODIGE 7: CRS alone vs CRS + HIPEC (oxaliplatin), HIPEC did NOT improve OS. Benefit of HIPEC persists only selectively in low PCI (<11).
MONOGRAPH 5 Anal Canal Tumors

5.2 SCC & Nigro Protocol

Nigro Protocol: 50.4 Gy + 5-FU + mitomycin C (MMC). Do NOT biopsy before 26 weeks post-CRT.
MONOGRAPH 6 Fournier's Gangrene

6.1 Operative Atlas: Radical Debridement

FGSI > 9: Mortality ~75%. FGSI ≤ 9: Mortality ~9%.

Mandatory return to OR at 24–48 hours, disease always underestimated at first debridement.
