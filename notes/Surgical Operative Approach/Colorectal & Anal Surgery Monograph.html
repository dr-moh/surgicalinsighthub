Surgical Resection — When Endoscopy Fails

  • Laparoscopic right hemicolectomy: ileocolic, right colic, right middle colic divided; CME with D3 for malignant polyps
  • Proctocolectomy + IPAA (J-pouch): definitive FAP operation age 15–25; staged 2 or 3-step
  • IRA (ileorectal anastomosis): AFAP with <20 rectal polyps; lifelong rectal surveillance q6–12 months
  • Subtotal colectomy: Lynch syndrome with synchronous lesions or young patient

1.3

Board Review / Viva

Viva 1
T1 adenocarcinoma, Kikuchi Sm2, moderate differentiation, no LVI, margin 1.5 mm. Operate or surveil?
Low-risk T1 — Sm2 with clear margin (>1 mm), no LVI, moderate differentiation. ESGE/BSG support surveillance: colonoscopy at 3 months, then 1 year.
Viva 2
16-year-old, APC mutation, 200+ adenomas, 12 rectal polyps. What operation?
Proctocolectomy + IPAA. IRA acceptable only if <20 rectal polyps and patient strongly prefers; requires 6-monthly flexible sigmoidoscopy for life. IPAA is oncologically superior here.
Viva 3
22-year-old FAP patient develops 8 cm intra-abdominal mass. No CRC. Diagnosis and management?
Desmoid tumour (mesenteric fibromatosis) — leading cause of death in FAP post-colectomy. Treat with sulindac + tamoxifen first-line; imatinib/sorafenib for progression. Avoid surgery unless obstruction/life-threatening — trauma triggers growth.
Viva 4
35 adenomas, APC negative. Next test?
MUTYH-associated polyposis (MAP). Test biallelic MUTYH mutation. Autosomal recessive. CRC lifetime risk ~80%.
Viva 5
MSI-H Stage II T2N0M0 sigmoid cancer. Adjuvant chemotherapy?
No adjuvant therapy. MSI-H Stage II has favourable prognosis; 5-FU is potentially harmful in dMMR tumours. Pembrolizumab not approved in adjuvant Stage II setting.
MONOGRAPH 2 Colon Cancer (CRC)
2.1

Operative Atlas — Complete Mesocolic Excision

Key Anatomical Landmarks

  • Toldt's fascia: embryological fusion plane — the correct CME dissection plane; avascular, preserves ureter and gonadal vessels
  • Right ureter: crosses right iliac vessels at pelvic brim, lateral to ileocolic pedicle; lift mesentery medially to identify
  • SMV: medial landmark for right hemicolectomy; ileocolic vein defines vascular pedicle
  • Left ureter: posterior to IMA origin — always identify before IMA ligation
  • IMV: ligated at inferior pancreatic border in left hemicolectomy for tension-free anastomosis
  • Hypogastric nerves: arise at IMA origin; injured during high IMA ligation if dissection goes posterior to pre-aortic fascia → ejaculatory dysfunction
★ CME PrincipleHohenberger 2009: Sharp dissection in embryological fascial planes + central vascular ligation at mesenteric root (D3) + intact mesocolic envelope. RELARC trial 2024 confirmed DFS benefit of CME vs D2.

Board Review / Viva

Viva 1
Right-sided obstructing CRC, no perforation, no metastases. Management?
Right-sided obstruction — primary resection and anastomosis safe. Laparoscopic right hemicolectomy. No role for SEMS right of hepatic flexure (technically difficult, high perforation risk).
Viva 2
T3N1a MSS, KRAS mutant, CEA normalised. Adjuvant therapy?
Stage IIIA. FOLFOX × 6 months or CAPOX × 3–6 months. MSS excludes pembrolizumab. KRAS excludes anti-EGFR. Adjuvant bevacizumab not proven (AVANT, QUASAR 2 — negative).
Viva 3
Post-op day 4 fever + abdominal pain after left hemicolectomy. CT shows pneumoperitoneum. Clavien-Dindo grade?
Anastomotic leak requiring laparotomy = Clavien-Dindo IIIb.

Neoadjuvant Therapy & TNT Trials

ESMO Risk Stratification & Treatment

Very low / lowT1–2 N0, CRM clearPrimary surgery

Key TNT Trials

RAPIDO (Lancet Oncol 2021)SCRT 25Gy/5fx + 18wks CAPOX/FOLFOX → surgery vs LCRT → surgery. TNT reduced disease-related treatment failure (23.7% vs 30.4%, HR 0.75). pCR 28% vs 14%.
PRODIGE 23 (NEJM 2021)mFOLFIRINOX 6 cycles → LCRT → surgery vs LCRT → surgery. 3-yr DFS 75.7% vs 68.5%. pCR 27.5% vs 12%.
OPRA (JCO 2022)Consolidation chemotherapy after LCRT → higher cCR and organ preservation rates (58% vs 43%) at 3 years.

Watch-and-Wait — Exam Essentials

  • cCR definition: No residual tumour on MRI (TRG 1, fibrosis only) + no ulceration/mass on endoscopy + normal CEA
  • Surveillance: DRE + flexible sigmoidoscopy Q3M × 2 years, then 6-monthly; MRI pelvis Q6M
  • Regrowth rate: ~25–30% at 2 years; ~95% of regrowths are local (salvageable by TME)
Exam trapcCR ≠ pCR. 15–20% of apparent cCR have residual tumour at histology. Discuss both W&W and surgery options at MDT.

LPND — Eastern vs Western

  • Eastern (Japan): Routine bilateral LPND for T3–4 below peritoneal reflection. JCOG0212: LPND reduces lateral LR (7.4% vs 12.6%); no OS difference.
  • Western: LPND only if nodes ≥7mm pre-CRT remain ≥4mm post-CRT. LCRT sterilises lateral nodes; LPND adds urinary/sexual morbidity.
3.3

Board Review / Viva

Viva 1
T2N0 low rectal cancer 2 cm from dentate, CRM clear 8 mm. Operation?
Options: APR (sphincter sacrifice) or ISR (intersphincteric resection) with ultra-low hand-sewn coloanal anastomosis. Decision: sphincter bulk on anorectal manometry, continence baseline, patient preference. No neoadjuvant for T2N0 CRM-clear.
Viva 2
CRM 0.5 mm anteriorly on MRI, T3c. What before surgery?
Threatened CRM — neoadjuvant CRT mandatory (LCRT or SCRT + consolidation). Reimage 8–12 weeks post-CRT. If still threatened — consider pelvic exenteration or ELAPE.
Viva 3
cCR after TNT. At 18-month surveillance, nodular irregularity at tumour site on sigmoidoscopy. What next?
Local regrowth (~25%). Biopsy to confirm viable tumour. If positive → salvage TME. Achieves similar R0 rates to primary surgery; sphincter preservation possible in ~60%.
Viva 4
LARS score >29 (major LARS) at 6 months post-ileostomy closure. Management pathway?
Dietary modification → loperamide → transanal irrigation (highest evidence) → biofeedback → sacral neuromodulation (SNM) for refractory major LARS.

MONOGRAPH 4 CRLM & Peritoneal Disease
4.1

Resectability & FLR

  • Adequate FLR: ≥20–25% normal liver; ≥30–40% post-chemotherapy/cirrhotic liver
  • Clear surgical margin (R0, ≥1 mm)
  • Preserve ≥2 contiguous hepatic segments with adequate inflow, outflow, and biliary drainage
  • No unresectable extrahepatic disease
FormulaFLR% = (FLR volume) / (Total functional liver volume − tumour volume) × 100

4.4

Board Review / Viva

Viva 1
Synchronous sigmoid cancer + 6 bilobar hepatic lesions, RAS mutant. FOLFOX + bevacizumab proposed. Surgical strategy?
Assess resectability at baseline and after 2–4 cycles. Options: primary-first, liver-first, or simultaneous. Cease bevacizumab ≥6 weeks before hepatectomy. PVE if FLR <25% post-chemotherapy.
Viva 2
PCI 18 peritoneal disease, no extrahepatic disease. Patient asks about CRS/HIPEC.
PRODIGE 7 showed oxaliplatin HIPEC did not improve OS over CRS alone. CRS with CC-0 improves survival vs chemotherapy (40+ vs 16 mos). Refer to specialist centre; PCI 18 is borderline for CC-0. Realistic expectations essential.
Viva 3
Pre-hepatectomy oxaliplatin rules?
Cease ≥4 weeks before surgery; assess for sinusoidal obstruction syndrome (SOS) via LFTs.

5.3

AIN / HSIL & Melanoma

GradeOld TermWHO TermManagement
AIN ILow-gradeLSILObservation; no treatment required
AIN IIHigh-gradeHSILTreat if symptomatic or immunocompromised
AIN IIIHigh-gradeHSILActive treatment: WLE or ablation
ANCHOR trial (NEJM 2022): Treatment of HSIL in HIV+ patients reduces progression to invasive anal SCC by 73% (HR 0.27).

Anorectal Melanoma

  • <1% of anorectal tumours; 5-yr OS ~10–20%; often amelanotic
  • Surgery: WLE (R0, 1 cm margin) vs APR — multiple series show no OS difference; WLE preferred for QoL
  • Systemic: Pembrolizumab/nivolumab for metastatic; BRAF V600E (~30–40%) → encorafenib + binimetinib
5.4

Board Review / Viva

Viva 1
T2N1 anal SCC (right inguinal node). Treatment plan?
Nigro protocol (50.4 Gy + 5-FU + MMC). Inguinal node included in elective nodal irradiation field (bilateral groins). No upfront surgery. Assess response at 8–12 weeks. Biopsy at 26 weeks if residual. Salvage APR if persistent.
Viva 2
Renal transplant patient on tacrolimus with AIN III perianal. Management?
High-risk — up to 10%/year progression in immunosuppressed. ANCHOR trial supports active treatment. HRA-guided ablation or WLE if localised. Staged ablation if >30% circumference to prevent stricture.
Viva 3
How to distinguish cloacogenic carcinoma from rectal adenocarcinoma histologically?
Cloacogenic: p63+, CK5/6+, CEA−/low; HPV-associated; treated as anal SCC (Nigro). Rectal adenocarcinoma: CK20+, CDX2+, CEA+; treated as rectal cancer (surgery ± neoadjuvant CRT).

6.3

Emergency Protocol

  • Resuscitation: 2 large-bore IV; 500 mL crystalloid bolus; noradrenaline if MAP <65 despite 1–2 L.
  • Antibiotics: Piperacillin/tazobactam (4.5g 8-hrly) + Metronidazole + Vancomycin (MRSA). Do not delay for cultures.
  • Imaging: CT pelvis only if haemodynamically stable. Gas tracking along fascial planes is diagnostic.
  • Post-op ICU: Nutrition (early EN), glycaemic control (6-10 mmol/L), VAC therapy once clean, STSG at 2-4 weeks.
