Mastering the Core Surgical Training (CST) Portfolio: A UK Recruitment Strategy Guide

Career Guide UK Recruitment June 11, 2026

A strategic breakdown of the new letter-grading CST portfolio system, key recruitment timelines, and actionable tactics to score Grade A in every domain.

Editorial Review & Clinical Commentary This article is an expanded strategic review and clinical commentary on the original guide "Core Surgical Training (CST) Portfolio Guide" written by Leo Gundle (Plastic Surgery Registrar, Guy's and St Thomas' Hospital, London) and published on Medset.co.uk. In compliance with copyright guidelines, we summarize the timeline and point frameworks while adding professional development insights from the Surgical Insight Hub editorial staff.

UK Core Surgical Training (CST) recruitment remains one of the most competitive pathways in postgraduate medical education. Securing a coveted CT1/ST1 surgical post requires not only clinical acumen and solid MSRA performance but also a highly optimized, verified training portfolio. For the 2025/2026 recruitment cycle, the portfolio evaluation system has undergone a major transition, moving away from simple numerical scoring toward a comprehensive **Letter-Grading Evaluation System (A to E)** across key domains.

Under this revised structure, assessors evaluate portfolios based on specific tiers of evidence, with candidates receiving a final portfolio score at the interview stage based on both their uploaded documents and responses to portfolio-focused questions. Understanding these guidelines and planning early is essential to maximize your score and avoid technical disqualifications.

📊 Domain Grading Matrix (Grades A–E)

Each portfolio section is assessed and assigned a letter grade. Below is a detailed matrix summarizing the requirements for each tier across the five key domains:

1. Operative Experience

Grade A: 40+ Cases

Requirements & Evidence: Candidates must submit a verified consolidation report generated from the official eLogbook system. This report must include a case count summary and date ranges, alongside a consultant signature, printed name, GMC number, and date. Only cases where the candidate was scrubbed as "Assisting", "Supervised - Scrubbed", or above count toward the total. Observational roles are invalid.

Surgical Insight Strategy: Hitting 40+ cases can be challenging, particularly during busy medical foundation rotations.
  • Negotiate Theatre Time: Proactively discuss with your clinical supervisors to secure half-days in theatre during light ward periods. Offer to complete discharge summaries early in exchange for scrub opportunities.
  • Log Appropriately: Ensure you are actively assisting. Do not log cases where you were merely observing. Focus on minor, high-volume procedures (e.g., abscess drainages, appendectomies, hernia repairs, or wound debridement) where you can easily get hands-on experience.
  • Prioritize Efficiency: Once you reach the 40-case milestone, redirect your attention to other domains of your portfolio where points are outstanding. Excess cases beyond 40 do not yield higher grades.

2. Surgical Experience

Grade A: Taster or Elective

Requirements & Evidence: In the updated framework, points are no longer awarded for standard foundation training rotations in surgery. To achieve Grade A, you must upload a letter on official hospital letterhead from your supervisor confirming completion of either a surgical elective (4+ weeks) or a surgical taster week (5+ days) in an accepted surgical specialty (e.g. General, Trauma & Orthopaedics, Plastic, Urology, ENT, Vascular, Neurosurgery, OMFS, Cardiothoracic, Paediatric, or ITU - valid for 2025/26 only).

Surgical Insight Strategy: Because standard rotations no longer score points, scheduling a taster week is now a mandatory strategy for the vast majority of applicants.
  • Plan Early: Do not leave taster days until the end of FY2. Apply for study leave and arrange taster days early in FY1 or the start of FY2.
  • Pick a Strategic Specialty: Choose a specialty that aligns with your career goals, but also one where you can easily get a consultant supervisor to verify your attendance.
  • Secure the Letter Promptly: Draft the supervisor letter yourself, ensuring it includes the department details, exact dates, GMC number, and hospital logo, so the consultant only needs to review and sign it.

3. Quality Improvement Projects (QIP) & Audits

Grade A: Surgically-Themed Closed-Loop

Requirements & Evidence: Scoring in this domain is split into QIP Involvement and QIP Presentation. To achieve Grade A for involvement, you must lead all aspects of a surgically-themed audit or QIP (including project design, data collection, and implementing an intervention) spanning two or more cycles (closed-loop) with demonstrated improvement. Evidence requires slides or a written summary, plus a consultant letter confirming the project met these criteria.

Surgical Insight Strategy: Audits take time. A closed-loop QIP with an intervention and re-audit cycle typically takes 3 to 6 months to complete.
  • Choose a surgically-themed topic: To get Grade A, the topic must relate to surgical patients, theatre pathways, or perioperative care. General medical audits will limit you to Grade B.
  • Design Simple Interventions: Focus on simple, high-impact changes (e.g., introducing a new poster, updating a local surgical ward checklist, or giving a short educational presentation to nursing staff). This makes the second cycle data collection straightforward and demonstrates a clear change.
  • Double-Claiming Warning: You cannot use the same project to score points in both the QIP section and the publications/presentations section. Select your best surgical project for QIP, and use other work for presentations.

4. Presentations & Publications

Grade A: First-Author PubMed or National Prize

Requirements & Evidence: Grade A is awarded for either winning a top prize for an oral presentation at a national/international meeting, or being the first author of a PubMed-indexed journal article (excluding case reports or editorials). Evidence must include the PubMed ID, a copy of the publication, or official conference programs showing your name as presenter and the national scope of the meeting.

Surgical Insight Strategy: Peer-reviewed publishing can have long timelines.
  • Leverage Collaborative Research: Join established surgical trainee research collaboratives (e.g., STARSurg, GlobalSurg). While collaborative authorship often lands you in Grade D, it is a reliable backup.
  • Target Trainee Conferences: Submit abstracts to trainee-focused national conferences (such as the ASiT or BOTA annual conferences). They have higher abstract acceptance rates and offer opportunities to present posters or short oral papers.
  • Consultant Mentorship: Approach academic surgical registrars or consultants. Ask if they have existing datasets that require analysis and writing. They are often looking for keen juniors to draft papers.

5. Teaching Experience

Grade A: ≥4 Face-to-Face Sessions

Requirements & Evidence: Achieving Grade A requires organizing and personally delivering at least four face-to-face teaching sessions to peers or undergraduates. You must submit a consultant-signed verification letter confirming your role and the delivery dates, alongside formal, anonymized feedback forms summarizing participant responses.

Surgical Insight Strategy: Setting up a teaching course is one of the easiest ways to secure a Grade A if approached systematically.
  • Organize a Medical Student Series: Contact the undergraduate coordinator at your hospital and set up a 4-week bedside teaching or MRCS preparation course for clinical medical students.
  • Maintain Structured Feedback: Create a simple feedback form (e.g., using Google Forms or printed feedback sheets). Collect feedback after each session, compile the results, and have your consultant review and sign off on the feedback summary.
  • Train the Trainers Courses: Attending accredited courses (such as the Train the Trainers or Teach the Teachers courses) can support your teaching portfolio and qualify you for Grade D even if you lack independent teaching hours.
Critical Portfolio Warnings

Patient Identifiable Data (PID): Any document uploaded to the recruitment portal containing patient names, initials, NHS numbers, or date of birth will result in an immediate score of zero for that domain, and may lead to a formal GMC referral. Always double-redact your eLogbooks, case summaries, and QIP documents before uploading.

Electronic Signatures: Avoid using copy-pasted or editable digital signatures. Assessors are increasingly rejecting evidence with unverified electronic signatures. Always obtain handwritten signatures or secure GMC-verified email confirmations.

⚡ Quick Portfolio Checklist

Ensure your evidence is flawless before the February deadline:

No PID Redact names, hospital numbers, and specific dates from logbooks and audit slides.
Official Letterheads All supervisor sign-off letters must be on formal NHS Trust or University letterhead.
GMC Numbers Ensure every consultant signature includes their printed name and active GMC number.
